Our Accessibility Principles

We believe accessibility is not just a feature — it's a fundamental right. Here’s how we make our digital experience inclusive for all.

Clear Structure

We build websites with logical content hierarchy and intuitive navigation to simplify browsing for all users.

Assistive Tech Friendly

Our content is optimized for screen readers and supports various assistive technologies.

Readable Design

We ensure high contrast, clean fonts, and scalable text for easy reading and visual comfort.

Keyboard Accessibility

All features and interactions are fully operable using only a keyboard.

Descriptive Media

Images and videos include meaningful alt text, captions, and transcripts where needed.

WCAG Compliance

We follow global accessibility standards like WCAG to meet and exceed compliance requirements.

No Harmful Animations

We avoid flashing/strobing content and offer the option to reduce motion where possible.

User Feedback

We value your feedback and are committed to ongoing improvement in accessibility and usability.

Accessible Forms

We use clear labels, error messages, and focus indicators to make forms fully accessible.

Responsive Accessibility

Our designs adapt to all screen sizes and devices while maintaining accessibility standards.

Skip Links

We include skip-to-content links and landmarks for quick keyboard navigation.

Theme Customization

We plan to offer options for high contrast and dark mode themes to suit user needs.

Why Accessibility Matters

At Hisho & Kanri, accessibility isn’t just a checkbox — it’s a commitment to equity, inclusion, and better user experience. We believe everyone deserves equal access to information, services, and opportunities, regardless of ability.

From navigating our website to interacting with our accounting and compliance solutions, we want every user to feel supported and empowered. A truly accessible experience builds trust, reduces frustration, and shows respect for every individual’s unique needs.

We proudly serve a wide range of businesses and professionals across Singapore, Malaysia, and India, and our solutions are designed to be inclusive for all — including those with:

  • Visual impairments (low vision, color blindness, blindness)
  • Auditory impairments (hearing loss, deafness)
  • Motor challenges (limited mobility, tremors, alternative navigation tools)
  • Cognitive differences (learning disabilities, memory or focus-related conditions)

Creating accessible digital spaces is part of our ongoing mission to be a dependable, ethical, and forward-thinking professional firm. We're continually learning, listening to feedback, and improving our accessibility efforts to reflect evolving global standards.

“Accessibility is not a feature, it’s a foundation. When we design for inclusion, everyone benefits.”
